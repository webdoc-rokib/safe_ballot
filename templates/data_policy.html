Data Policy

Effective date: August 29, 2025

1. Purpose

This Data Policy summarizes how SafeBallot stores, retains, and deletes data related to elections, accounts, and backups.

2. Encrypted ballots

Ballots are stored as encrypted ciphertexts. Retention of ciphertexts must be governed by organizational policy because re-encrypting or rotating keys without reprocessing ballots will render older ballots irrecoverable. If you choose to retain ballots long-term, store keys in a KMS and retain access logs.

3. Account data and PII

Account PII (email, phone) should be retained only as long as necessary for the purposes of the election and auditing (suggested default: 1 year). After the retention period PII should be deleted or anonymized.

4. Backups

Backups must be encrypted, access-controlled, and rotated according to the retention schedule. Ensure that backup expiration results in the deletion of sensitive copies.

5. Deletion

Deletion of ciphertexts and backups is required to fully remove ballots; simply deleting user accounts does not remove ballots that are stored anonymously. Deletion operations must be logged and audited.

6. Access control

Access to keys, backups, and audit logs must be restricted to authorized personnel and logged. Use role-based access control in production environments.

7. Legal and compliance

Follow jurisdictional requirements for data retention when operating in production. Consult legal counsel for public elections.

8. More detail

See DATA_RETENTION.md in the repository for a more detailed retention schedule and operational guidance.
